Human-robot interaction · assistive robotics · open-source platforms

Allison (Amy) O'Connell

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Southern California, advised by Prof. Maja Matarić. My work focuses on modular, low-cost, open-source robots for in-the-wild human-robot interaction studies.

I have contributed to projects including Blossom and the NIH Smart Health project.

Research

Accessible robot platforms

Designing modular, low-cost, open-source robots that make human-robot interaction studies easier to run in the wild.

Personalized assistive systems

Work on the NIH Smart Health project on AI-driven models for supporting user engagement and adherence in health interventions.

AR/VR for robot interaction
