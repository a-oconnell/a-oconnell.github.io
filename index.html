Human-robot interaction · assistive robotics · open-source platforms

Robotics, human-robot interaction, and accessible research platforms.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Southern California, advised by Prof. Maja Matarić. My work focuses on modular, low-cost, open-source robots for in-the-wild human-robot interaction studies.

I contribute to Blossom, the NIH Smart Health project, and AR/VR interfaces for proxemics and robot signal design.

About

I work at the intersection of human-robot interaction, socially assistive robotics, and accessible tools for research and education. My dissertation focuses on iterative development of modular, low-cost, open-source robots for in-the-wild HRI studies.
